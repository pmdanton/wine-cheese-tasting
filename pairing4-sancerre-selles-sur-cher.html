Pairing 1: Sancerre and Selles sur Cher

Wine

Sancerre, a renowned white wine, comes from the eastern part of the Loire Valley in central France. The region's terroir is defined by its cool climate, along with its diverse soils, which include limestone, flint, and clay. Sancerre is made primarily from Sauvignon Blanc grapes, which thrive in the region's unique conditions. The wine is characterized by its crisp acidity, vibrant fruit flavors, and pronounced minerality, which are all hallmarks of the Loire Valley's terroir. A classic Sancerre offers flavors of grapefruit, lemon, green apple, and gooseberry, along with subtle herbaceous and floral notes. The wine's high acidity and lean structure make it an excellent food companion, particularly for goat cheese, seafood, and light poultry dishes.

Cheese

Selles sur Cher is a traditional French goat cheese originating from the Loire Valley. Made from raw goat's milk, this artisanal cheese is characterized by its unique, flat-topped cylindrical shape and a delicate ash-ripened rind, which forms during the aging process. The cheese is aged for a minimum of three weeks, during which its flavors intensify and its texture becomes increasingly dense and creamy. Selles sur Cher offers a tangy, slightly earthy flavor profile, with notes of fresh herbs, citrus, and a hint of minerality. The cheese's complex taste is a result of the diverse vegetation found in the Loire Valley, where the goats graze.

Pairing Experience

The pairing of Sancerre and Selles sur Cher brings together two regional delicacies, creating a harmonious and invigorating tasting experience that highlights the best of the Loire Valley's terroir. The acidity and citrus flavors of the Sancerre balance the tanginess of the Selles sur Cher, enhancing the freshness and vibrancy of both the wine and the cheese. The wine's herbaceous and floral notes complement the cheese's earthy and herbaceous flavors, while the Sancerre's pronounced minerality echoes the subtle minerality found in the Selles sur Cher. This pairing showcases the natural affinity between the Loire Valley's Sauvignon Blanc wines and its exquisite goat cheeses, offering a delightful taste of the region's rich culinary heritage.
