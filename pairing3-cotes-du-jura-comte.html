Pairing 3: Côtes du Jura and Comté

Wine

Côtes du Jura is an appellation in the Jura wine region in eastern France, renowned for its unique and diverse range of wines. Made from Chardonnay grapes, this white wine embodies the distinctive character of the region's cool climate and diverse soils (marl, limestone, and clay). Côtes du Jura Chardonnay often undergoes a process known as oxidative aging, which results in complex flavors and a distinct nutty character. The wine's tasting profile may include flavors of green apple, lemon, butter, hazelnut, and a touch of vanilla. Depending on the winemaking style, some versions may also exhibit "sherry-like" notes due to the oxidative aging process. Its moderate to high acidity and full body make it an excellent partner for a range of cheeses.

Cheese

Comté, a beloved French cheese, hails from the Franche-Comté region, located in the eastern part of the country near the Swiss border. Made from raw cow's milk, Comté is a firm, pressed cheese that undergoes a lengthy maturation process, typically ranging from four to 24 months. The cheese's flavors develop and become more complex as it ages, with younger Comté offering a fruity and slightly sweet profile, while older Comté boasts notes of caramel, hazelnuts, and a subtle earthiness. The diverse flora of the Jura Massif, where the Montbéliarde and French Simmental cows graze, contributes to Comté's rich and varied taste. The cheese's texture is smooth and supple, with small, evenly distributed holes called "eyes."

Pairing Experience

Pairing Chardonnay Côtes du Jura with Comté creates a harmonious union of flavors that exemplifies the unique character of the Jura region. The wine's bright acidity and apple and lemon flavors offer a pleasing contrast to the cheese's caramel and nutty notes, while its buttery and vanilla nuances complement the cheese's creamy texture. The wine's potential oxidative character further enhances this pairing, echoing the complex flavors found in well-aged Comté. This pairing invites a deep appreciation for the Jura's distinctive terroir and time-honored traditions.
