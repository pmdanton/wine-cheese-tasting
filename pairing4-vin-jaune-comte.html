Pairing 4: Jura Vin Jaune and Comté

Wine

Jura Vin Jaune, a distinctive and rare wine, is produced in the Jura region of eastern France, nestled between Burgundy and Switzerland. The region's terroir is characterized by its cool climate, high altitude, and diverse soils, including limestone, marl, and clay. Vin Jaune, made primarily from the indigenous Savagnin grape variety, undergoes a unique aging process. It is aged under a layer of yeast, called "voile," in partially filled oak barrels for a minimum of six years and three months. This oxidative aging method imparts complex flavors of toasted nuts, dried fruits, and spices to the wine, along with a savory, almost umami-like character. Jura Vin Jaune is known for its intense golden color, full body, and high acidity, which contribute to its exceptional aging potential.

Cheese

Comté, a beloved French cheese, hails from the Franche-Comté region, located in the eastern part of the country near the Swiss border. Made from raw cow's milk, Comté is a firm, pressed cheese that undergoes a lengthy maturation process, typically ranging from four to 24 months. The cheese's flavors develop and become more complex as it ages, with younger Comté offering a fruity and slightly sweet profile, while older Comté boasts notes of caramel, hazelnuts, and a subtle earthiness. The diverse flora of the Jura Massif, where the Montbéliarde and French Simmental cows graze, contributes to Comté's rich and varied taste. The cheese's texture is smooth and supple, with small, evenly distributed holes called "eyes."

Pairing Experience

The pairing of Jura Vin Jaune and Comté unites two regional specialties, creating a rich and complex tasting experience that showcases the unique terroir of eastern France. The nutty and savory flavors of the Vin Jaune harmonize with the nuttiness of the Comté, while the wine's high acidity and dried fruit notes provide a pleasant contrast to the cheese's creamy, caramel-like sweetness. The full-bodied Vin Jaune adds depth and warmth to the tasting, and its oxidative character complements the earthy, mature flavors of aged Comté. This pairing highlights the best of the Jura region's terroir, as well as the exceptional craftsmanship involved in the production of both Vin Jaune and Comté.
