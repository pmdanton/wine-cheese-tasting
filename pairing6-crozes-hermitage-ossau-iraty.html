Pairing 6: Crozes-Hermitage and Ossau-Iraty

Wine

Crozes-Hermitage is a well-regarded red wine from the Rhône Valley in southern France. Produced primarily from the Syrah grape, the wine's flavors can range from ripe red and black fruit to more earthy and savory notes. The vineyards' granitic soils lend a mineral quality to the wine, while maturation in oak barrels can impart a subtle hint of spice and vanilla. Its medium to full body, moderate acidity, and tannic structure make Crozes-Hermitage an excellent wine to pair with a variety of cheeses.

Cheese

Ossau-Iraty is a traditional cheese made in the Pyrénées region of southwestern France. It's crafted from the milk of sheep that graze on the diverse flora of the mountainous landscape. The cheese is semi-soft, with a creamy, smooth texture and a distinctive, slightly nutty flavor. As it matures, Ossau-Iraty develops complex flavors reminiscent of hay and dried fruit. The rind is naturally formed and edible, often exhibiting a distinct color gradient from yellow to orange.

Pairing Experience

Pairing Crozes-Hermitage with Ossau-Iraty creates a harmony of flavors that bridges the Rhône Valley and the Pyrénées. The wine's fruitiness and spicy undertones complement the cheese's nutty, slightly sweet profile, while its tannins offer a pleasant contrast to the creaminess of the Ossau-Iraty. Additionally, the mineral quality of the Crozes-Hermitage mirrors the earthy notes found in the cheese, creating a pairing that's balanced and layered with complexity. This tasting experience beautifully illustrates the synergies between regional French wines and cheeses.
