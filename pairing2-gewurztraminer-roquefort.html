Pairing 5: Gewurztraminer "Vendanges Tardives" and Roquefort

Wine

Gewurztraminer "Vendanges Tardives": Gewurztraminer "Vendanges Tardives", or "Late Harvest" Gewurztraminer, comes from the Alsace region in northeastern France. The Gewurztraminer grape itself is known for its fragrant, aromatic qualities, with typical notes of rose, lychee, and exotic spices. The term "Vendanges Tardives" means that the grapes were left on the vine longer than usual to accumulate more sugars, resulting in a richer, more concentrated wine. This wine offers a unique balance between sweetness and acidity, with layers of flavor including ripe fruits, honey, and sometimes a hint of smoky minerality. The Alsace terroir, with its combination of sunny, dry climate and diverse soils (volcanic, granite, limestone, and sandstone), greatly influences the distinct aromatic profile and complexity of Gewurztraminer "Vendanges Tardives".

Cheese

Roquefort, one of the world's most famous blue cheeses, originates from the village of Roquefort-sur-Soulzon in the Occitanie region of southern France. Made from raw sheep's milk, Roquefort is aged for a minimum of three months in the natural limestone caves of Combalou, where the mold Penicillium roqueforti thrives. This unique aging environment gives the cheese its characteristic blue veining, piquant flavor, and distinctive aroma. Roquefort is known for its crumbly, moist texture and a complex taste profile that combines a strong, tangy creaminess with a subtle sweetness and notes of grass and herbs. The cheese's flavors are a result of the rich, diverse vegetation of the Causses plateaus, where the Lacaune sheep graze.

Pairing Experience

The pairing of Gewurztraminer "Vendanges Tardives" and Roquefort brings together the aromatic richness of Alsace with the distinct character of southern France's Roquefort. The exotic fruitiness and residual sweetness of the Gewurztraminer balance beautifully with the bold, tangy, and slightly salty flavors of the Roquefort. Meanwhile, the wine's hint of minerality complements the cheese's creamy texture. The wine's bright acidity and floral aromatics keep the palate refreshed, enhancing the complexity of both the wine and the cheese. This pairing is a remarkable celebration of the diverse terroirs and time-honored traditions of France's gastronomy.
