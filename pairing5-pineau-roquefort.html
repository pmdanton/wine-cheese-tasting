Pairing 5: Pineau des Charentes and Roquefort

Wine

Pineau des Charentes is a unique fortified wine from the Charente and Charente-Maritime departments in southwestern France. Produced by blending unfermented grape must with Cognac eau-de-vie, this regional specialty has a rich history dating back to the 16th century. The terroir of the region, characterized by a temperate oceanic climate and diverse soils (limestone, clay, and sandy), contributes to the wine's fruity and aromatic qualities. Pineau des Charentes can be made from a variety of grapes, including Ugni Blanc, Colombard, and Folle Blanche for white Pineau, and Cabernet Franc, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Merlot for red Pineau. The wine is typically aged in oak barrels, which imparts flavors of caramel, vanilla, and toasted nuts. Pineau des Charentes offers a sweet, balanced palate with notes of honey, ripe stone fruits, and a touch of citrus.

Cheese

Roquefort, one of the world's most famous blue cheeses, originates from the village of Roquefort-sur-Soulzon in the Occitanie region of southern France. Made from raw sheep's milk, Roquefort is aged for a minimum of three months in the natural limestone caves of Combalou, where the mold Penicillium roqueforti thrives. This unique aging environment gives the cheese its characteristic blue veining, piquant flavor, and distinctive aroma. Roquefort is known for its crumbly, moist texture and a complex taste profile that combines a strong, tangy creaminess with a subtle sweetness and notes of grass and herbs. The cheese's flavors are a result of the rich, diverse vegetation of the Causses plateaus, where the Lacaune sheep graze.

Pairing Experience

The pairing of Pineau des Charentes and Roquefort brings together two iconic products of French gastronomy, showcasing a delightful contrast of flavors and textures. The sweetness and fruity notes of the Pineau des Charentes balance the bold, salty, and tangy flavors of the Roquefort, while the caramel and vanilla undertones of the wine complement the cheese's creamy richness. The wine's light acidity and touch of citrus help cleanse the palate, enhancing the appreciation of both the Pineau des Charentes' complexity and the Roquefort's bold taste. This combination of flavors highlights the unique terroir of the Charente region and the exceptional aging conditions of the Roquefort caves.
