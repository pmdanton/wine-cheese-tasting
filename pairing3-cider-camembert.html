Pairing 3: Cider and Camembert

Wine

Normandy Cider is a traditional fermented beverage made from apples, which are abundant in the Normandy region of northwestern France. The region's terroir, characterized by its temperate oceanic climate and fertile soils, is ideal for apple cultivation. Normandy Cider typically offers a crisp, slightly sweet profile with flavors of fresh apples and a gentle effervescence. Its moderate alcohol content and refreshing acidity make it an excellent food companion, particularly for rich, creamy cheeses.

Cheese

Camembert de Normandie, an iconic French cheese, originates from the Normandy region. Made from raw cow's milk, this soft, creamy cheese is characterized by its edible bloomy rind and a rich, buttery flavor. The cheese is aged for a minimum of three weeks, during which its flavors develop and its texture becomes increasingly velvety. Camembert de Normandie offers a complex flavor profile, with notes of earthiness, mushrooms, and a hint of barnyard, all underpinned by the cheese's natural creaminess. The unique taste of Camembert de Normandie is a result of the lush, nutrient-rich pastures in the region, where the cows graze.

Pairing Experience

The pairing of Normandy Cider and Camembert de Normandie creates a delightful contrast of flavors and textures, showcasing the best of the Normandy region's terroir. The cider's acidity and fruitiness cut through the creaminess of the Camembert, providing a pleasant contrast that highlights the distinct flavors of both the beverage and the cheese. The cider's effervescence acts as a palate cleanser, refreshing the taste buds and enhancing the enjoyment of the Camembert's rich, earthy flavors. This pairing offers a taste of Normandy's culinary heritage, highlighting the region's exceptional apples and the artisanal craftsmanship involved in the production of Camembert de Normandie.
